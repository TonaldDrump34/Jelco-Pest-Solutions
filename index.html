Deep Dive

Roaches

Cockroaches are resilient pests that thrive in warm, moist environments and can contaminate food with bacteria, triggering allergies or asthma. Signs include sightings at night, droppings like black specks, egg cases, or a musty odor. Professional treatment is essential, as they spread pathogens and reproduce quickly.

Bed Bugs

Bed bugs are small, reddish-brown insects that feed on human blood at night, causing itchy welts and allergic reactions (though they don't spread diseases). Look for rusty spots on bedding or live bugs in mattress seams. Early professional intervention prevents widespread infestations, especially after travel.

Termites

Termites silently destroy wood from the inside, causing billions in structural damage annually by feeding on cellulose. Signs include mud tubes on walls, hollow-sounding wood, or droppings (sawdust-like droppings). Annual inspections and professional barriers are key to protecting your home.

Mice

House mice are small rodents that gnaw on wiring (risking fires) and contaminate surfaces with droppings. Signs include droppings, gnaw marks, or nocturnal scratching sounds. Sealing entry points and professional exclusion prevent health risks.

Ants

Ants invade homes in trails seeking food, with some species like carpenter ants damaging wood structures. They can contaminate surfaces and sting painfully in certain types. Signs include visible trails, small soil mounds, or nests near foundations. Baiting and sealing cracks provide effective long-term control.

Spiders

Most house spiders are harmless and help control other insects, but some like black widows or brown recluses can deliver painful or dangerous bites. Signs include webs in corners, egg sacs, or sightings in dark areas. Reducing clutter and professional treatments minimize encounters.
